## The 8-File Structure Explained

### 00_overview.md
**Purpose**: The hook. Why should anyone care?
- State the thesis in one paragraph
- "Why now" section (what changed to make this possible)
- Index of what's in the other files

### 01_death_of_old_model.md
**Purpose**: Burn the ships. The old way is dead.
- Name the old model explicitly
- 3 failure modes (transfer, context, motivation work for most)
- Show the loop users are trapped in

### 02_product_as_category.md
**Purpose**: Define what you're actually selling
- Not a product pitch - a category definition
- Layers/architecture if technical
- Why this is a NEW thing, not an improvement

### 03_progression_system.md
**Purpose**: How users "win"
- What does completion look like?
- How is progress tracked?
- Why self-reporting works (or doesn't)

### 04_economics.md
**Purpose**: Why this is viable NOW
- Cost structures (old vs new)
- Subsidies, margins, sustainability
- Who can afford this and who can't

### 05_origin_story.md
**Purpose**: Your credibility and the struggle
- What you tried that failed
- The pivot moment
- Why you're the one to build this

### 06_democratization.md
**Purpose**: The vision beyond early adopters
- Current barriers
- Phases of expansion
- End state vision

### 07_complete_picture.md
**Purpose**: Synthesis for the skimmers
- Full stack diagram (ASCII art)
- Comparison table (old vs new)
- The one-liner
- Why this is the future

## Quality Checklist

Before considering the thesis complete:

- [ ] Can read 00_overview.md and understand the entire thesis
- [ ] 01 makes you never want to use the old model
- [ ] 02 clearly defines what the new thing IS
- [ ] 03 has a concrete "win" condition
- [ ] 04 has real numbers (even estimates)
- [ ] 05 is honest about failures, not just successes
- [ ] 06 has phases, not just "everyone will use it"
- [ ] 07 has the one-liner

## Anti-Patterns

**Don't:**
- Write 8 files that say the same thing differently
- Skip the economics (viability matters)
- Hide the failures in your story
- End without the one-liner
- Make it about features instead of transformation

**Do:**
- Each file should add new information
- Be specific about costs and constraints
- Show the whole journey including failures
- Synthesize into a memorable one-liner
- Focus on user transformation, not product features
