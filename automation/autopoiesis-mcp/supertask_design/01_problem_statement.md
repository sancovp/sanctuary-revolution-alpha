# Problem Statement: Ralph Loops Are Not Enough

## User's Words (Verbatim)

> "ralph is GOOD if you are running it in solo runs and it forgets what happened last run. But as soon as it remembers, it is doing tasks. But it seems to me like everyone else is using it for tasks and i just wonder if the humans who are excited by the ralph loops are actually just retarded noobs?"

> "you cannot use the technology to make better technology, then what is the point?"

> "the new thing then is that ralph is just a prompt telling you to be honest.... but thats where i got the victory-promise idea in the first place."

> "Now the other thing we have to do is change it from PROMISE DONE to promise ABSOLVED. That way you can only undo a promise when you are formally absolved. That never happens, so you never exit. Thats an infinite loop -- not just 'here is the be honest text again and the tasks'. SUPERTASK + VOW + ARE YOU ABSOLVED OF THE VOW?"

## Commentary

Ralph loops as commonly used are task runners with a conscience check. "Did I do the thing? Be honest." That's useful but not transformative.

The insight: changing DONE to ABSOLVED fundamentally alters the exit condition. DONE is self-assessed. ABSOLVED requires external authority. You cannot absolve yourself.

Formula: SUPERTASK + VOW + ABSOLUTION
